Why I Play

Why I Play

I picked up the guitar when I was a teenager, drawn in by the way a few simple chords could capture emotions that words alone couldn't express. What started as plucking out simple melodies quickly became a deep passion. The guitar is my creative outlet, my stress reliever, and one of the ways I connect with the world around me.

Over the years I have explored folk, classical, fingerpicking, and a little bluegrass. Each style has taught me something new about music — and about patience, practice, and persistence.

A Brief History of the Guitar

The guitar's roots stretch back over 4,000 years to ancient instruments found in Egypt and Mesopotamia. The modern guitar evolved through European lutes and vihuela during the Renaissance, before Spanish luthiers shaped the classical acoustic guitar we recognize today in the 19th century.

  1. Ancient Origins (2000 BC – 1400 AD)
    • Tanbur and oud instruments used across the Middle East
    • Lute brought to Europe by Moorish musicians
  2. Renaissance & Baroque (1400 – 1750)
    • Vihuela flourished in Spain and Italy
    • Four-course and five-course guitars gained popularity
  3. Classical Period (1750 – 1900)
    • Six-string guitar standardized by Antonio Torres
    • Fernando Sor and Mauro Giuliani elevated concert guitar
  4. Modern Era (1900 – Present)
    • Electric guitar invented in the 1930s by Adolph Rickenbacker
    • Rock, blues, jazz, and country transformed music globally
    • Guitar remains the world's most popular instrument
